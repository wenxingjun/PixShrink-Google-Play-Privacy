# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated:** February 23, 2026

PixShrink (“we,” “our,” or “us”) respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how PixShrink collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use the PixShrink mobile app (the “App”).

If you do not agree with this Privacy Policy, please do not use the App.

## 2. Information We Access or Process

### A. Photos and Image Metadata (on-device)
When you grant photo access, the App can read photos you select from your device to provide compression features.

The App may access image-related metadata (for example: location/GPS, camera/EXIF, capture date, copyright metadata) to support metadata removal options you choose.

**Important:** Image compression and metadata handling are performed on your device. We do not operate our own server to upload your photos for compression.

### B. Google Sign-In Information
If you sign in with Google, the App may receive your basic profile information (such as display name and email address) from Google Sign-In and store it locally in app preferences for account display and feature flow.

### C. Purchases and Subscription Status
If you buy Pro features, purchase processing is handled by Google Play Billing. We may process purchase status data returned by Google Play to unlock or maintain Pro access.

### D. Ads-Related Data (Google AdMob / Google services)
The App uses Google Mobile Ads (AdMob). Google and its partners may process data such as device/app identifiers (including advertising identifiers where applicable), IP address, diagnostics, and ad interaction data for ad delivery, measurement, fraud prevention, and related ad operations.

### E. App Preferences and Local History
The App stores preferences locally (for example theme, language, notification vibration/sound settings, and Pro state indicators) and compressed history references in app storage.

### F. Feedback Email Information
If you tap **Feedback**, the App opens your email app (Gmail when available) and pre-fills email content. The footer includes:
- device model
- Android version
- PixShrink app version

This information is sent only if you choose to send the email.

## 3. Permissions We Request

- **READ_MEDIA_IMAGES / READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE**: to let you pick and process photos.
- **INTERNET**: required for ads, Google sign-in, and billing communication.
- **com.google.android.gms.permission.AD_ID**: used by Google ad services for ads-related functionality.
- **com.android.vending.BILLING**: required for Google Play in-app purchases/subscriptions.
- **VIBRATE**: used for optional in-app feedback vibration.

## 4. How We Use Information

We use data to:
- provide photo compression and metadata options,
- save your settings and app state,
- enable Google sign-in and account display,
- process Pro entitlement status via Google Play Billing,
- show ads and rewarded ads (free plan),
- improve app stability and prevent abuse/fraud via platform services,
- respond to feedback messages you send.

## 5. Data Sharing

We do **not sell** your personal data.

We may share/process data with third-party service providers only as needed for app functionality:
- **Google Play Services / Google Sign-In**
- **Google Play Billing**
- **Google AdMob (ads)**

Your use of those services is also subject to Google’s own policies.

## 6. Data Retention and Deletion

- **Local app preferences/history** are kept on your device until you clear app data/cache, sign out (where applicable), or uninstall.
- **Compressed outputs** saved to your gallery remain until you delete them from your device.
- **Google account display data in app** can be removed by signing out.
- **Cache** can be removed via the in-app **Clear Cache** feature.
- Purchase/accounting records related to billing may be retained by Google under Google Play policies.

## 7. Security

We use reasonable measures to protect local app data and rely on trusted platform/SDK channels for network communications. However, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

## 8. Children’s Privacy

PixShrink is not directed to children under 13 (or the relevant digital age in your region). If you believe a child has provided personal information through the App, contact us and we will take reasonable steps to address it.

## 9. International Processing

Third-party providers (such as Google services) may process data on servers in multiple countries according to their own privacy frameworks.

## 10. Your Choices

You can:
- control permissions in Android system settings,
- sign out of Google within the App,
- clear cache via **Settings > Clear Cache**,
- stop use by uninstalling the App,
- manage ad-related settings through your Google/device settings where available.
